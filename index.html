Popular Destinations

1. Bodh Bihar

Bodh Gaya, Bihar, India: A pilgrimage site of profound spiritual significance, Bodh Gaya is where Buddha attained enlightenment. Visit the awe-inspiring Mahabodhi Temple, meditate under the sacred Bodhi tree, and explore diverse international monasteries. Immerse yourself in a cultural exchange, embrace tranquility, and embark on a journey of self-discovery in this ancient town.

2. Patna

Patna, the capital city of Bihar, India, is a vibrant blend of history, culture, and modernity. As one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, Patna offers a rich tapestry of attractions.Stroll along the banks of the Ganges River, where ghats come alive with religious rituals and vibrant markets offer local delicacies and handicrafts.

Read More

3. Nalanda

Nalanda, in Bihar, India, is a testament to ancient wisdom and scholarship. As an UNESCO World Heritage Site, it attracts visitors with its rich history.Discover ancient monasteries, temples, and libraries, showcasing the architectural grandeur of the Gupta period.

Read More

4. Vaishali

Vaishali ,Bihar, India, is steeped in history and spirituality. Here's a glimpse of what this ancient city offers.Marvel at the famous Ashoka Pillar, adorned with intricate carvings and inscriptions, commemorating Emperor Ashoka's visit to Vaishali.

Read More

5. Rajgir

Rajgir, nestled in the scenic landscapes of Bihar, India, is a captivating destination blending spirituality, history, and natural beauty.Explore sacred sites like the Vishwa Shanti Stupa and Griddhakuta Hill, where Lord Buddha preached the Fire Sermon.Embark on outdoor adventures like trekking, boating, and wildlife spotting in the scenic surroundings of Rajgir.

Read More

6. Madhubani

Madhubani, located in the Mithila region of Bihar, India, is renowned for its vibrant art and cultural heritage. Explore the town's rich tradition of Madhubani art, characterized by intricate designs, bold colors, and themes inspired by mythology and nature.Discover ancient landmarks like the Janaki Temple, dedicated to Goddess Sita, and the Rajnagar Palace, offering insights into the region's royal history.

Read More

7. Sitamarhi

Sitamarhi, located in Bihar, India, holds immense religious significance as the birthplace of Goddess Sita from the Hindu epic Ramayana. Enjoy the serene surroundings of Sitamarhi, with lush greenery, tranquil water bodies, and scenic landscapes perfect for peaceful retreats and meditation.Discover historical landmarks such as the Haleshwar Sthan, an ancient Shiva temple with intricate carvings and architectural splendor.

Read More

8. Valmiki National Park

Valmiki National Park, located in Bihar, India, is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. Discover the park's cultural significance, with ancient temples and historical sites like the Valmiki Ashram, associated with the epic Ramayana.Valmiki National Park offers a unique blend of wilderness, adventure, and cultural heritage, making it an ideal destination for eco-conscious travelers seeking immersive wildlife experiences.

Read More

9. Bhagalpur

Bhagalpur, situated in Bihar, India, offers a blend of historical, cultural, and natural attractions.Known for its silk production, Bhagalpur is a hub of silk weaving and trading, offering insights into the traditional craft.Visit revered sites such as the Mandar Parvat, associated with Hindu mythology, and temples like Bateshwar Nath Temple for spiritual solace.

Read More

10. Muzaffarpur

Muzaffarpur invites travelers to delve into its cultural tapestry, indulge in its culinary delights, and explore its historical and spiritual landmarks.Discover ancient temples and religious sites such as Baba Garib Sthan Mandir and Shri Ram Temple, offering moments of serenity and devotion.Participate in cultural festivals and events, such as the Shahi Litchi Mela, celebrating the city's agricultural bounty and cultural heritage.
